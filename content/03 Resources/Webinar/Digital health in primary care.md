# Digital health in primary care
## Why digital health?
- reduce inefficiencies
- improve access
- reduce costs
- increase quality
- make medicine more personalized
- ADVANCE HEALTH EQUITY

Digitalization =/= turning our steps into just binary zeroes and ones
It is a major opportunity to actually evolve our model of care!

How?

## Barriers and challenges
- unreliable electricity
- unreliable network
- unreliable devices
- digital illiteracy 
- upfront cost

-> causes the **digital divide** -> all of the sudden you have **digital determinants of health** (f.e. covid pandemic where all information was online)

## For who?
Why not start digital health interventions for those who need it the most
-> f.e. refugees, mobile populations -> maybe people on the move can become the owner of their own health record

# Digital health equity
How to implement AI so it can 
- prevent disparities
- address barriers
- promoting inclusion
- creating global impact
- safeguarding sustainability

-> and not counterwork this!!
-> High-level AI challenges without equity may exacerbate existing inequities (CAVE [[Goal misgeneralization]])
-> Dozens of sources of bias, not only coming from data

## Digital x Global heaalth
- many challenges - infrastructure, geographical, cultural and language barriers
- also a lot of potential -> key take-away -> *AI has the power to transform healthcare in low resource systems ONLY if it is implemented responsibly*

### Concrete examples
- DeepMalaria -> AI for malaria drug discovery -> recognize most promising compounds
- SkinVision -> AI for skin cancer detection

## Risks of AI in health equity
- algorithmic bias (biased data can amplify disparities)
- lack of transparency (black-box models)
-> huge responsibility of policy makers and healthcare providers -> embed equity from the very beginning

# Digital healthcare in humanitarian contexts
## Changing trends in humanitarian response
- more people are displaced
- duration of responses is longer
- financial support/attention not growing accordingly
- fragile and conflict-affected areas are growing faster
-> capacity response is being tested -> can digital health solutions be a solution here?

MSF sees use primarily in diagnostic tools and event detection

Examples: 
- mobile phone devices and handheld microscopes as diagnostic platforms for malaria and NTD's -> CAVE often these new technologies don't make it to low resource settings
- MinION -> Whole-Genome Sequencing in resource-limited settings (just usb in laptop)
- antibiogo -> identify resistance mechanism smartphone based (resistant, sensitive, intermediate)
- Community bases surveillance -> outbreak prevention/early intervention

CAVE trust in the organization/healthcare provider needs to stay essential -> the implementation of these tools must not take this away -> makes people more vulnerable for 'unsafe' sources of information

# Pro
- building up a habit of accountability
- using your privilege as someone earning top 20% money for something good, taking responsibility for the massive inequality in the world, which is not your fault no, but you benefit from it 
- living intentionally

# Con
- giving money to a good cause often does not do justice to the complexities of reality; are you just incentivizing a status-quo?
	-  doing nothing also incentivizes the status quo
- cost-effectiveness?
	-> meta-charities [^1]
	- [One For The World](https://1fortheworld.org)
	- [Giving What We Can](https://www.givingwhatwecan.org)
	- [The Life You Can Save](https://www.thelifeyoucansave.org)
- there are other ways of doing good
	- yes ofcourse, so let's do them all!
		- positive social impact through career or personal life
		- advocacy and activism

# Footnotes
[^1]: A meta-charity is an organization that doesn’t seek to help people in need directly, but seeks to help many potential donors to start helping people in need directly, and to help them more effectively.

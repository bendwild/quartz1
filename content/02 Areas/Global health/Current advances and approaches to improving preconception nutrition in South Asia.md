# South Asian context
- rapidly developing
- poor child health
	- however 25% of the worlds children, yet 50% of the worlds wasted children live in Asia
	- 52% of live births are small, vulnerable children
- poor health of women (fe low BMI for women throughout their life course)
	- lowest indicators of women’s status and empowerment globally
	- relatively high levels of women’s education, yet low labor force participation

# Gender inequality in women’s nutrition
- poor services
- low decision-making power
	- policies and programs regarding women’s nutrition programs are low priority and lack funding
- poor quality diets
	- women eat last and least in terms of nutritious, quality food


> [!ATTENTION] Consequences of malnutrition across a woman’s life course
> - short stature
> - impaired cognitive development
> - consequences for infants and children
> 	- low birthweight
> 	- [[Prematuriteit]]

## Supplementation
- especially during critical periods like pregnancy
- **multiple micronutrient supplementation**
	- UNIMMAP MMS
	- 15 essential vitamins and minerals for pregnant and nursing women
	- meets micronutrient requirements that poor diets cannot meet
	- a switch from historic iron & folate supplement (before 2020 global policy guidance used IFA)
		- transitioning from IFA to MMS can avert over 250 000 child deaths and nearly 25 million disabilities, assuming 30% coverage across LMICs
- what about preconception period however?
	- most guidelines only recommend MMS during pregnancy
	- in South Asia, more than half of women don’t engage with health systems **until the second trimester of pregnancy**
	- intervening during preconception period is advisable

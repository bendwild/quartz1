# Wat?
= neglected tropical disease
- caused by onchocerca volvulus
- transmitted by blackflies
	- breeding site in fast-flowing rivers
- global burden 
	- 22 million infected
	- 220 million at risk
	- 99% in SSA

Adult worms in nodules produce microfilariae that migrate to: 
- skin → itching & skin lesions
- eyes → river blindness
- CNS? → epidemiologically linked to epilepsy

Epidemiological definition (≠ clinical definition → pathogenesis remains unknown)
1. Onset of seizures between 3-18 years old
2. >3 years residence in onchocerciasis-endemic village with
	1. high epilepsy prevalence
	2. household clustering of epilepsy
3. Normal psychomotor development prior to seizure onset
4. NO obvious cause of epilepsy

Other arguments in favour
- head nodding seizures (nodding syndrome)
- severe unexplained stunting with delayed or absence of external signs of sexual development (Nakalanga syndrome)

# Pathogenesis
→ unknown

What is known?
- only 1 known risk factor: microfilarial load
- OAE is preventable
	- CDTI 
		- mass drug administration
		- once/twice a year 
		- > 5 years old (not in pregnant woman, contra-indicated)
		- ivermectin kills mf in the skin
	- Vector control
		- larviciding
		- slash & clear

Further research
- virome of O. volvulus (contains OVRV1, need to understand impact of OVRV1 on pathogenesis)
- HLA as potential co-factor for OAE (already association found with Nodding syndrome)

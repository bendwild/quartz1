> [!info] Base question
> Who gets the outcome (disease) in the exposed & unexposed

# Prospective cohort study

![[Pasted image 20251005131206.png]]
1. Define cohort (population)
2. Determine who has disease or not (prevalent cases)
	1. CAVE VN/VP
3. Determine exposure **in those who do not have the disease**
	1. Who is exposed? 
	2. Who is not?
4. Follow forward in time (incident (new) cases)
	1. Who develops the disease?
	2. Who does not develop the disease?

![[Pasted image 20251005125752.png]]
> [!ATTENTION] Know your limits!
> - not good for **rare diseases**
> - not good for diseases with **long latency** (fe [[Longkanker]])
> - usually very expensive
> - losses to follow-up
> - exposure is often not constant!


# Retrospective cohort study
1. Define cohort (population) **that existed in the past**
2. Determine who has disease or not (prevalent cases)
	1. CAVE VN/VP
3. Determine exposure (in those who do not have the disease)
	1. Who is exposed? 
	2. Who is not?
4. **Pretend to** follow forward in time (incident (new) cases)
	1. Who develops the disease?
	2. Who deos not develop the disease?

![[Pasted image 20251005130202.png]]


> [!ATTENTION] Know your limits!
> - depends on the **availability of past records**
> - exposure is often not constant

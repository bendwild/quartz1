# Definition
Epidemiology is the study of **how disease is distributed in populations and the factors that influence or determine this distribution.** Basically; “Why does a disease develop in some people and not in others?”

Broader definition: The study of the distribution and determinants of health-related states or events in specified populations and the application of this study to control of health problems

# Objectives
- identify **etiology** and relevant **risk factors**
	- to direct preventive effors
	- to modify risk factors
- develop a rational basis for prevention programs and serve as the foundation for developing public policy
	- 2 approaches: population-based or high-risk targeting
- determine the extent of **disease found in the community (burden)**
- study the **natural history and prognosis of disease**
- evaluate both existing and newly developed preventive and therapeutic measures and modes of health care delivery
- provide clues to changes that take place over time in the health problems presenting in the community

→ ultimate goal is to **improve the control of disease** through both prevention and treatment

# History
 - [[@Hippocrates]]
	 - introduced concepts of endemic vs epidemic diseases
	 - suggested environmental factors as key causes of diseases
- [[@Ibn Sina Avecinna]]
	- ideas on contagion and quarantine
	- role of water and air in disease spread
- Smallpox inoculation in ancient China (as early as the 10th century)
- [[@Sushruta Samhita]]
	- foundations for surgery
	- importance of sanitation in preventing disease spread
- [[@ Al-Razi]] (scientist, Islamic world)
	- differentiated between smallpox and measles
	- emphasized clinical observation and disease classification
- Quarantine practices during black death (Venice)
	- miasmic theory
 - [[@Ignaz Semmelweis]] and hand hygiene intervention in childbed fever
 - [[@Edward Jenner]] and smallpox (vaccine)
 - [[@John Snow]] and cholera
	 - traced the source of a cholera outbreak in London in 1854 to a contanimated water pump

> [!INFO] 
> The likely explanation for the decline in mortality that we have seen in the past centuries are **improvements in social conditions**, rather than medical advancements

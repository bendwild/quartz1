> [!INFO] What is bias?
> Any systematic error (≠ random errors/noise) occurring during design, conduct or analysis of the study

|                                   | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | Pathways                                                                                      | Mitigation                                                                                                                                                                                                 | Example                                                                                                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Selection bias**                    | = systematic difference between <br>- those who participate in the study and those who do not (generalizability - **external validity**)<br>- those in the treatment arm of a study and those in the control group (comparability between groups - **internal validity**) | - loss to follow up<br>- variables that is a common effect on both exposure and outcome       |                                                                                                                                                                                                            | ![[Pasted image 20251021111235.png]]                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Information bias**                  | = errors in measurement of exposure, outcome or confounding variables<br>- recall bias<br>- interviewer bias<br>- misclassification bias                                                                                                                                  | - information is gathered differently for different groups<br>- measurements are not accurate |                                                                                                                                                                                                            |                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Misclassification bias**            | = when a participant is placed into a wrong group/category<br>- differential (information errors differ between groups)<br>- non-differential (information is incorrect but is the same across groups)                                                                    | - observational or measurement error                                                          |                                                                                                                                                                                                            | ![[Pasted image 20251021111513.png]]                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Interviewer bias<br>Observer bias** | = when researchers unintentionally influence the outcome based on their **expectations or knowledge** of the exposure/disease status of the participants                                                                                                                  | - particularly common with subjective outcomes (pain, psychological measures)                 | - use blinding (masking)<br>- use objective/standardized measures to assess outcomes                                                                                                                       | ![[Pasted image 20251021111853.png]]                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Recall bias**                       |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |                                                                                               |                                                                                                                                                                                                            |                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Lead-time bias<br>Latency bias**    | = when early detection of disease (f.e. screening) gives the **false impression that survival has improved** (even though the course of the disease has not changed)<br>- time between detection and disease (latency) is lengthened<br>- endpoint (death) stays the same |                                                                                               | - adjust for lead time in survival analysis (time of diagnosis relative to the time relative to the time of clinical manifestation of the disease)                                                         | If a screening program detects cancer earlier than usual, patients may appear to live longer after diagnosis, even if the treatment doesn’t change the actual course of the disease. |
| **Survivorship bias**                 | = when studies only include participants who survived a certain period of time<br>- CAVE overlooking those who died/dropped out the study<br>- overestimation of survival rates<br>- underestimation of risk                                                              |                                                                                               |                                                                                                                                                                                                            | ![[Pasted image 20251021112259.png]]                                                                                                                                                 |
| **[[Hawthorne's effect]]**            | = when participants change their behaviour simply because **they know they are being observed**                                                                                                                                                                           |                                                                                               | - use unobtrusive measurement techniques<br>- try to make monitoring routine/natural                                                                                                                       | ![[Pasted image 20251021112341.png]]                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Publication bias**                  | = when studies with positive/statistically significant results are more likely to be published                                                                                                                                                                            |                                                                                               | - encourage publication of negative results<br>- pre-register study protocols to ensure transparency<br>- use meta-analytic techniques that account for potential publication bias (f.e. **funnel plots**) | If studies showing no association between an exposure and a disease are not published, the literature will falsely suggest that the association is stronger than it is.              |

> [!ATTENTION] External vs Internal validity
> - Internal validity: “Does the [[study design]], conduct and analysis **answer the research question without bias**?” (design, errors and biases)
> - External validity: “Can the study findings be **generalized** to other contexts?” (robust random sampling)

> [!ATTENTION] Direction of bias
> - Bias towards the null (usually introduced by **non-differential misclassification**, however not always predictable)
> - Bias away from the null

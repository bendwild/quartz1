> [!INFO] What is a confounder?
> 1. The confounding factor must be **associated with both risk factor of interest and outcome**
> 2. The confounding factor must be **distributed unequally among the groups being compared**
> 3. The confounding factor **cannot be an intermediary (mediator) step** in the pathway

# Impact
- may account for the entire/part of the apparent association
	- positive confounding (overestimation of the association)
	- negative confounding (underestimation of the association)

## By design
1. Restriction
	1. reduces the number of subjects
	2. residual confounding can occur if you don’t restrict narrowly enough
	3. you can’t evaluate the effects of factors that have been restricted for
	4. restriction limits generalizability
2. Matching
	1. can be time-consuming and expensive
	2. limits sample size
	3. can’t evaluate the effect of the factors you matched for
3. Randomization (in clinical trials/intervention studies only)

--- column-break ---

## By statistical analysis
1. Stratification
2. Multiple variable regression analysis

> [!ATTENTION] Effect Modifier / Interaction
> Both "effect modification" and "interaction" are terms used in [[epidemiology]] and statistics to describe situations where the effect of a primary exposure on an outcome varies by levels of another variable.

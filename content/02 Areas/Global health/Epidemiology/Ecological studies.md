> [!info] Basic principles
> - unit of analysis is population/group rather than the individual
> - exposure is only assessed for a group or population, no data on exposure on each individual in the group

> [!ATTENTION] Pitfalls
> - Exposure misclassification
> 	- some of the individuals in our exposed group are actually unexposed
> 	- think of chocolate nobel prize winners example
> - Ecologic fallacy
> 	- assumption that all members of a group exhibit characteristics of the group at large
> - Factors other than the risk factor of interest be responsible for observed association

> [!Check] Usually easier and less expensive than other designs

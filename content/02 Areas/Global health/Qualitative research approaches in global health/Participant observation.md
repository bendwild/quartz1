# Notes
## What is it?
- qualitative method with the objective to **learn the perspectives, contexts, behaviors… held by those you are studying**
	- multiple perspectives
	- communities are not homogenous

## How
- observing
	- document and feel what daily life is like
- participating
	- get immersed in activities
- combination

> [!attention] Outsider perspective
> Participant observation always happens from an outside perspective, even though the researcher might be an insider

## Why
- no substitute for witnessing the unfolding of daily life to gain in-depth understanding
- especially useful for hard-to-reach populations (hidden, highly mobile, transient)

### Strengths
- deep insight into contexts, relationships, behaviors
- can generate information previously unknown to researchers

--- column-break ---

### Con
- time consuming
- researcher dependent (memory, diligence)
- requires conscious effort of objectivity/awareness **positionality** and **interpretive lens**

> [!INFO] Example
> Why do high rates of HIV persist after clean needle campaign?
> - participant observation showed “indirect sharing”

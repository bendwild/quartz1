The study’s purpose and methods
- What was the study design?
- What was the primary purpose of the study?
	- to understand the role of emotional, informational and tangible social support for Ugandan women affected by fistula
- Was the purpose and rationale clear and compelling, to you? Why or why not?
	- social support is an important mechanism to build resilience to health challenges
	- hypothesis based on framework of Berkmann and Glass
	- for individuals with other chronic diseases, social support has been shown to assist
	- research among women with fistula has found that social support helps, however **key social support mechanisms and pathways are not well elucidated**, hence a qualitative review of women’s experiences in Uganda
- Where was the study conducted?
	- Mulago National Teaching and Referral Hospital, Kampala, Uganda
- Who was interviewed? How were they selected?
	- 33 participants who had had fistula surgery 6-24m prior to the study from Mulago Hospital in Kampala, Uganda
	- interviews and FDGs conducted by same female Ugandan interviewer, with an additional moderator present for FDGs
- What methods were used?
	- used data from another qualitative study (El Ayadi et al. 2017; Byamugisha et al. 2015; El Ayadi et al. 2019)
	- in-depth interview (16)
	- focus group discussion (4 with 17 participants)
		- CAVE only post-repair
	- sociodemographic questionnaire
	- research team comprised of Ugandan and US maternal health clinicians and researchers

The presentation of results
- What were the main findings of the study?
	- various individuals were key providers of social support across different domains (family, friends, community organisations and other women affected by fistula)
	- social support was critical in helping women cope with fistula, access fistula care and post-repair recovery
	- women relied heavily on tangible and emotional support to meet their physical and psychological need
	- participant narratives highlighted the importance of social support in managing and recovering from fistula
	- support-enhancing interventions for women and their families may be a promising strategy for improving the experiences and QoL
- How are the findings organized in the paper? What were some strengths or weaknesses to the way the findings were presented?
	- socio-demographic and reproductive characteristics of study participants
	- types and sources of social support (emotional, financial, tangible, informational)
	- impact of social support (access to care, resilience, coping, reintegration)
	- gender role distinctions
- How do the authors support (show evidence for) their findings?
	- the importance of a broad support system including multiple individuals for meeting the varied needs of women with fistula is consistent with findings from other research
	- the use of an established theoretical framework that has been used in other similar studies = strength
	- secondary nature of analysis, limited geographic region and hospital, lack of appraisal domain (counterintuitive to '“use of an established theoretical framework”)

- What did the authors conclude from this work? 
	- the key role of social support for women affected by fistula for coping with
	- improving women’s timely access to high quality obstretic care is essential
	- social support interventions may be an important way to improve the quality of life of women with fistula
	- CAVE a lot of “just like other research discovered…”
- What recommendations did they make? 
	- further research to understand options for applying these findings within an intervention framework
	- further research within a variety of cultural settings

# Reflections

- psychological

		- chronicity of the disease
		- invisibility of the effects
	- self-image
		- blackness
		- fat
- personal relations
	- childhood/parents
	- partner

The societal stigma that is placed upon an individual with diabetes plays a huge role in their quality of life. It impacts their daily life, emotional impact and relationships. Societal stigma pertaining to other socio-demographic characteristics that an individual might belong to can intersect with diabetes, creating a complex web of expectations and false beliefs that is hard to unravel. The fact that diabetes is a chronic disease intensifies this dynamic, as it strongly influences the emotional development in different stages of life. Unhealthy coping mechanisms might be developed (as noted by participant one) which further impact relationships and emotional impact. While the categories are intrinsically interlinked, i see them as distinct enough to build upon further and develop a theory. Further themes might emerge as the data analysis continues.

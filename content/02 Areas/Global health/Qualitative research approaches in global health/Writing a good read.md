# Reflections
- one narrative style does not fit all
- determine the central point/story line of your research
- balance description, analysis and interpretation based on research purposes and methods (f.e. qualitative descriptive study vs [[Grounded theory]])
- determine whose point of view/voice will prevail in the write-ups
- use metaphors as a qualitative writer
- writers must choose which organization of findings render the data in the most illuminating, least complicated and non-redundant way (time, prevalence, sensitizing concepts and coding families)µ

> [!PDF|187, 97, 229] [[Sandelowski_Writing a good read.pdf#page=8&annotation=141R|Sandelowski_Writing a good read, p.8]]
> > There is nothing more wasteful of both researchers’ and participants’ efforts than having important findings lost in clichéd, overwrought, and disorderly prose.

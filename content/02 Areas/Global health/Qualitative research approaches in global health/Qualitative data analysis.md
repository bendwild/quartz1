# Core concepts
= think of coding as a system to organize your data
## Purpose of Analysis
- Stay close to the data while making meaning from it
- Create a systematic "filing system" for your findings
- Transform raw data into interpretable themes and patterns
- No standard system exists for coding, it depends entirely on your research question

## Coding Fundamentals
- Coding = assigning tags/labels to units of meaning in your data
- Creates organized system for identifying and retrieving findings
- Not about counting frequencies, but about understanding meaning and patterns
- Multiple coding options exist for the same passage (emotional, descriptive, sequential, frequency-based)

## Research Question as Your North Star
- Your research question determines which coding approach is most appropriate!!
	- [[Grounded theory]]
	- general inductive approach
	- [[Phenomenological inquiry]]
	- discourse analysis
	- content analysis
	- thematic analysis
	- [[Framework analysis]]
- Not all coding options are equally important to your specific research goals
- Always keep your research question in mind when making coding decisions

# Coding process and strategies

## Multiple Coding Approaches
- Same passage can be coded multiple ways if relevant to your research
- Example: "When I was a child, I did something exciting with my family" could be coded as memory, sequence of events, or emotional experience

## First Pass vs. Refined Coding
- Initial coding: Code passages multiple ways if they seem important
- Later refinement: Review codes and determine what's truly important
- Clean up and consolidate your coding scheme based on patterns

# Quality and rigor in qualitative research
## Subjectivity and Standards
- Qualitative work is inherently subjective
- Traditional quantitative standards (representativeness, reliability, generalizability) don't apply the same way
- Focus on thoroughness and transparency instead

## Memo Writing
**= critical for quality**
- Document your coding process thoroughly
- Track what you notice each day ("Today I noticed emotion," "Today I saw memory inconsistencies")
- Memo writing demonstrates reliability and transparency to readers
- Shows how you were honest and systematic with your data

## Iterative Process
- Continuous refinement of codebook as you work with data
- Revise definitions, combine similar codes, split overly broad codes
- Test coding consistency across team members if working collaboratively

> [!ATTENTION] Key questions to ask yourself
> - Am I doing this thoroughly?
> - Am I taking detailed notes about my process?
> - Am I constantly checking back with my research question?
> - Am I connecting back to my literature review?
> - Am I answering my research question?
> - Am I transparent about my methods
> - Can I demonstrate a systematic approach through documentation?

## Enhancing analytic rigor
- Multiple coders (discussions can provide insights and refine ideas)
- Member checking (respondents’ own reactions to key emerging findings)
- triangulation (using more than one method of data collection to increase **internal validity**)

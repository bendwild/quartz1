# Reflections
Selective sampling: 
- calculated decision to sample a specific locale according to a preconceived but reasonable initial set of dimensions (time, space, identity, power…) which are worked out in advance for a study

Theoretical sampling: 
- the process of data collection for generating theory whereby the analyst jointly collects, codes, and analyses their data and decides which data to collect next and where to find them, in order to develop his theory as it emerges
- ongoing process of data collection that is determined by the emerging theory and therefore cannot be predetermined
- sampling on the basis of concepts that have proven theoretical relevance to the evolving theory

Open sampling: 
- sampling those persons, places, situations that will provide the greatest opportunity to gather the most relevant data about the phenomenon under investigation

All sampling is purposeful in qualitative research!

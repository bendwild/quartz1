# Reflections
Some tips for interviewers: 
- assume that participant’s comfort level has higher priority than obtaining juicy data
- pay close attention as to when to probe (clues)
- try to understand the experience from the participant’s view and validate its significance to this person
- slant ending questions towards positive responses, no interview should end abruptly when the participant is distressed
- your notes are not there to pen everything down, they remind you to return to earlier points and suggest how to frame follow-up questions
- be reflexive about the nature of the questions you ask and the assumptions/perspectives you hold
- attend to respondent’s language so you can bridge their experience with your research questions

# Golden circle
< by [[@ Simon Sinek]]

→ we have three layers
- why
- how
- what

Most people start with the what → then how → but often don’t talk too much about why they do it

However, the biggest leaders usually start with the **why** → *start with why to inspire action* → what’s your purpose, call, beliefs **and why should anyone care**

![[Pasted image 20250716175650.png]]

→ start with why and stay with why
- why me?
	- who are you wand why are you telling this 
- why you? 
	- why is this idea important for the listener?
	- why should the listener care?
	- what is the added value for the listener?

# Workshop

**Improving global health through climate action and equity**

## Why me?

We are facing various existential threats in 2025: climate change, nuclear war, water security etc. Often people think about these things as one-dimensional. However that could not be further than the truth. As a medical doctor with background in global health  I came to this forum with one mission: to learn about climate action, equity and policy in order to adapt global health to the world of tomorrow. I believe climate change will be one of the biggest obstacles for our already strained healthcare sectors and will further exacerbate many of the already existing issues such as the for example health inequality. This is an issue that will affect us all and I hope you will 

## Common ground (why me & why you?)

There are not many things in this world that are truly universal, even in the globalised world of today. However, when we look at the intersection of climate change and health(care) we know for a fact that 99% of the world population breathe toxic air. Food, water, and vector borne disease transmission, such as [[malaria]], cholera, dengue fever, and Lyme disease, are on the rise and the climate crisis is influencing how pathogens develop and travel. When faced by such a universal threat, the me and you disappear, there is no north, no south, there is only us and I believe that collective action is the only way to fight this.

Since this requires collective action, I believe the best way to participate or add to the movement is to use your own talents. Are you someone who is a real go-getter then you can write a letter to your local representative. Are you someone who is very creative you can use those skills to maybe create art and inspire people.

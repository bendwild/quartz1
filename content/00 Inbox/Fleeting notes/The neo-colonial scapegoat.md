What is truly frightening here is how progressive language, such as the concept of colonialism, is co-opted in support of capitalist extraction or regressive political projects. Žižek makes this point with the example of the pushback against ‘colonial’ influences like gay rights in Africa. Anti-gay laws in Uganda are being passed with the discursive justification that gay liberation represents a form of neo-colonialism. Of course, this ignores Africa's history of sexual diversity, and the historical role colonial law played in enacting anti-gay legislation.

Centrally managed flow of reusable accesories throughout the hospital

Buffer stockpile for resilience & supply chain stability

Voorbeeld van Karolinska Universitat

A holistic, harmonised assessment framework is urgently needed
- growing demand for sustainable healthcare
- current sustainability assessments fall short
	- for example only focusing on greenhouse gas emissions
- the risk for unintended consequences
	- patient safety
	- extra burden for staff

Environmental impact of medical technologies
- greenhouse gas emissions 
	- 4.4% of global emissions
	- mainly from energy and gases
	- device-related -> holistic life-cycle based assessment is needed (packaging, transport and reprocessing)
- waste
	- 1-2% of urban solid waste
	- reprocessing also generates waste: PPE, chemicals, packaging
- water resources
	- water scarcity affects 34% of EU
- water pollution

Human impact of medical technologies
- antimicrobial resistance
	- = top global threat!!
	- poor reprocessing -> surface/water contamination
- workforce wellbeing
	- over 50% of staff report burnout
- water resources
- wastewater pollution

Economic impact of medical technologies
- adverse events
	- up to 12.6% of health spending in OECD linked to adverse events
		- longer stays, more treatment, reputational damage
- purchase costs
	- single-use devices require higher purchase volumes
- cost over the lifecycle
	- true costs include also transportation, cleaning, maintenance...

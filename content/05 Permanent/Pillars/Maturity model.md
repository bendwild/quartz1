Notes in my swamp grow and evolve, just like plants in nature. I utilize a [[Maturity model]] to describe the evolution of my notes. A maturity model can be viewed as a playful application of structured growth. 
Here is how they grow: 

## FLORA

### Germination stage
#### Beans 🫘
#level-0🫘 Beans can be anything; tiny sparks—initial thoughts, questions, or ideas that haven’t been developed yet.
#### Seedling 🌱
#level-1🌱 Early-stage ideas I’ve started exploring but are far from cohesive.

### Growth stage
#### Plant 🌿
#level-2🌿 Notes with substantial content and emerging patterns. These are works in progress but offer value for further development. 
#### Tree🌴
#level-3🌴 Fully matured, coherent pieces of thought—essays, reflections, or structured knowledge—requiring only occasional refinement.

### Withered stage
#### Fallen leaf 🍃
#level-4🍃 Outdated or partially obsolete notes, kept for historical reference or to revisit original ideas.

## FAUNA
#### Worm 🪱
#level-5🪱 Worms are visitors in my swamp — notes imported/extracted from other mediums (e.g. Reading highlights and notes). They don’t grow like other notes but add value as they are.
#### Insect 🐛
#level-6🐛 Notes that serve as navigational tools—collections, indexes, or curated lists that make the swamp easier to explore.
